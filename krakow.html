📍 Kraków · Poland

Kraków with the Family

Poland's royal capital — compact, walkable, and full of dragons. Two adults and a 3-year-old, no car needed.

May 6–10, 2026 · 4 nights Adults × 2 · Toddler 3yo No car — on foot + tram ~15–20°C · Gorgeous in May

Trip at a Glance

Kraków is Poland's most beautiful city — a royal capital that survived WWII intact, with a fairy-tale Old Town your 3-year-old will love.

The Big Draw

Kraków's Old Town is UNESCO-listed and almost entirely car-free. Everything is within walking distance — you'll barely need public transport.

The Dragon Factor

Wawel Hill has a real fire-breathing dragon statue (it breathes fire every few minutes). For a 3-year-old, this is basically magic.

Weather in May

Typically 15–20°C, sunny and gorgeous. May is arguably Kraków's best month — green, blooming, and not yet overrun by summer crowds.

Compactness

The entire Old Town + Kazimierz fits in a 30-minute walk. No car, no metro needed — even with a stroller, you can do everything on foot.

Currency

Polish złoty (PLN). ≈ 4.2 PLN = 1 EUR. Kraków is noticeably cheaper than Warsaw — great value for restaurants and attractions.

Toddler Rating

The Market Square (Rynek) is Europe's largest medieval plaza — vast open space, pigeons, horse carriages, and street performers. Toddler paradise.

🐉

The Dragon is the star attraction for a 3-year-old

The Smok Wawelski (Wawel Dragon) statue at the base of Wawel Hill breathes real fire every few minutes. There's also a Dragon's Den cave (Smocza Jama) to explore — 135 spiral steps down, but the fire-breathing dragon at the exit is pure theatre for little ones.

👟

You will walk everywhere

Old Town is almost entirely pedestrianised. The Planty park ring encircles it — you can walk the full perimeter in 40 minutes. From Wawel Castle to Kazimierz is a 15-minute stroll along the Vistula. Pack good shoes and bring the stroller for when the little one tires.

🌼

May is peak bloom

The Planty gardens and Błonia meadow are at their most beautiful in May. The city puts on a series of festivals — check what's on during your dates (Dragon Parade usually happens in late May, but there may be related events). Crowds are manageable compared to July–August.

Must-See Sights

Almost everything is walkable from the Old Town. Kraków rewards slow exploration — don't rush.

🏛
Main Market Square
Rynek Główny
Europe's largest medieval market square — vast, cobbled, and ringed with colourful townhouses, cafés, and the magnificent Cloth Hall. Street performers, pigeons, horse-drawn carriages, and a trumpet call from St Mary's Basilica every hour on the hour. A 3-year-old could happily spend hours here running, chasing pigeons and watching the world go by.
👶 Toddler loves it Outdoor Free ⭐ Start here
📍 Heart of Old Town · Always open · Buğgy-friendly (mostly flat cobbles)
🐉
Wawel Castle & The Dragon
Zamek Wawelski · Smok Wawelski
Kraków's iconic hilltop royal castle dominates the city skyline. The courtyard is free to enter and offers lovely views. The real draw for toddlers is the Dragon's Den cave (Smocza Jama) at the base of the hill — a spiral descent through a real cave, emerging at the fire-breathing dragon statue on the Vistula bank. The dragon breathes actual fire every few minutes. Absolute magic.
👶 Dragon = highlight of the trip Outdoor ⭐ Don't miss
📍 10 min walk from Market Square · Dragon's Den: daily Apr–Oct Courtyard free · Dragon's Den 5 PLN/person · Under 7 free
⚠ 135 steep spiral steps into the cave — stroller stays at the top. Carry the little one or use a carrier.
🌿
Planty Park
Planty
A beautiful green ring of gardens that completely encircles the Old Town — created when the medieval city walls were demolished in the 19th century. Shaded paths, benches, fountains, and several small playgrounds dotted along the route. Perfect for a morning stroll with the buggy or letting the little one run free between sightseeing stops.
Outdoor Free 👶 Great stroller route
📍 Surrounds the entire Old Town · Full circuit ≈ 40 min walk
🕍
Kazimierz — The Jewish Quarter
Kazimierz
Kraków's most atmospheric neighbourhood — beautifully restored 15th-century Jewish quarter now full of quirky cafés, vintage shops, street art, and excellent restaurants. A 15-minute walk from the Market Square along the Vistula. Very walkable, relatively flat, and great for a slow morning or evening with the stroller. The Szeroka Street square is particularly charming.
Outdoor Free to explore
📍 15 min walk from Rynek · Best for lunch or early evening
🌊
Vistula Riverfront
Bulwary Wiślane
Kraków's riverside promenade below Wawel is lovely in May — less busy than Warsaw's but charming. The path runs from the dragon statue along to Kazimierz. Cafés, kayak rentals (for parents if grandparents are on hand!), and a great view up to the castle. A natural connector between Wawel and Kazimierz.
Outdoor Free 👶 Easy stroller walk
⛏
Wieliczka Salt Mine
Kopalnia Soli Wieliczka
A UNESCO World Heritage site 14 km from Kraków — an extraordinary underground world of salt-carved chapels, lakes, and sculptures. The standard tourist route is 3.5 km and takes ~2.5 hours. For a 3-year-old it's a lot (many stairs, dark tunnels, long tour) — consider the shorter "Gnomes' Route" designed specifically for children aged 4+. Worth it if your toddler has good stamina and you use a carrier.
Underground ⭐ UNESCO landmark 📲 Book ahead
📍 Bus 304 from Kraków Główny · 30 min · Daily from 9:00 Adult ~89 PLN · Child ~72 PLN · Gnomes' Route shorter & cheaper
⚠ No strollers underground. Use a carrier. Minimum age for Gnomes' Route is technically 4 — assess your child.
🎨
Cloth Hall — Sukiennice
Sukiennice
The stunning Renaissance building in the centre of Market Square. Ground floor is a traditional craft market — amber jewellery, wooden toys, hand-painted ornaments. Perfect for picking up a souvenir wooden dragon for the little one. The upper floor has a small gallery of 19th-century Polish paintings.
Indoor 👶 Wooden toys! Market: free to browse
📍 Centre of Rynek Główny · Market open daily 10:00–18:00
🦁
Kraków Zoo
Ogród Zoologiczny w Krakowie
Smaller and less famous than Warsaw's zoo but set in a beautiful forest (Las Wolski) on the edge of the city. Around 1,400 animals — big cats, primates, elephants. The forest setting makes it feel more natural. Allow 3 hours and pair with a walk in the woods. Requires a tram or bus — not walking distance from Old Town.
👶 Kids love it Outdoor
📍 Tram 18 to Salwator, then bus 134 · ~40 min from centre Adult ~25 PLN · Child ~15 PLN · Under 3 free
⛪
St. Mary's Basilica & the Trumpeter
Bazylika Mariacka · Hejnał Mariacki
The magnificent Gothic church that dominates Market Square. Every hour, a trumpeter plays the famous Hejnał Mariacki melody from the taller tower — and cuts it off mid-phrase (a tradition since the 13th century, commemorating a trumpeter shot mid-call by a Mongol arrow). Kids absolutely love counting down to the trumpet and watching the red scarf wave from the tower window. Inside, the enormous carved wooden altarpiece by Veit Stoss is one of the greatest works of medieval art in Europe.
👶 Toddler loves the trumpet! Outdoor viewing free ⭐ Every hour on the hour
📍 Market Square · Interior Mon–Sat 11:30–18:00, Sun 14:00–18:00 Interior 15 PLN · Tower 25 PLN · Under 7 free
🎺 Stand in the square a few minutes before the hour for the best view of the tower window
🏯
Florian's Gate & the Barbican
Brama Floriańska · Barbakan
Walk through the only surviving medieval city gate — thick walls, arrow slits, and a dark stone passage your toddler will love charging through. Just outside stands the Barbican, a circular Gothic fortress (one of only three left in Europe) bristling with turrets. You can walk the short connecting wall between them. The whole thing takes about 30 minutes and is completely accessible with a stroller. A great intro to medieval Kraków.
Outdoor 👶 Castle walls + dark passages
📍 North end of Old Town · Floriańska St · 5 min walk from Market Square Barbican 15 PLN · Under 7 free · Gate exterior always free
⛰
Kościuszko Mound
Kopiec Kościuszki
One of Kraków's four ancient earthen mounds — this one built in 1823 to honour the Polish and American revolutionary hero Tadeusz Kościuszko. A gentle 15-minute walk up delivers a 360° panorama of the whole city, with the Tatra mountains visible on clear days. The climb itself is the adventure — toddlers love reaching the top. There's a small café at the base for a post-climb treat. The romantic stone fortifications around it are free to explore.
Outdoor 👶 Climb + panoramic views Free to climb
📍 Tram 18 to Salwator · 10 min walk up the hill · Always accessible Grounds & climb free · Fortifications museum 14 PLN
🏭
Schindler's Factory Museum
Muzeum Fabryki Schindlera
One of the most powerful and brilliantly designed history museums in Europe — the actual enamelware factory where Oskar Schindler saved over 1,200 Jewish workers during WWII. The permanent exhibition "Kraków Under Nazi Occupation" is immersive and emotionally unforgettable. Best visited by parents during the toddler's nap time with a carrier — there's no play space, but the building is stroller-accessible and the content is historically essential for adults. Allow 2–3 hours.
Indoor ⭐ Historically essential 📲 Book ahead — sells out
📍 ul. Lipowa 4, Zabłocie · Tram 3, 19, 24 · Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00 · Mon 10:00–14:00 Adult 32 PLN · Child 24 PLN · Mon: FREE (book anyway)

Parks, Playgrounds & Open Spaces

Kraków is surprisingly green and very stroller-friendly. The whole centre is essentially a giant pedestrian zone.

🌿
Planty Park Ring
Planty
The green belt encircling the entire Old Town. Multiple playgrounds along the route, plenty of benches, and beautiful May flowers. An ideal morning circuit with the buggy — combine with stops at the Market Square and Wawel along the way.
OutdoorFree👶 Playgrounds en route
🌾
Błonia Meadow
Błonia
A vast open meadow just west of the Old Town — 48 hectares of open grass, perfect for picnics and kite-flying. Kids can run without limits. In May it's vivid green and gorgeous. A 20-minute walk from the Market Square or short tram ride.
OutdoorFree👶 Unlimited running space
📍 15 min walk west of Old Town · Tram 15 or 18 to Cracovia
🎪
Jordan Park
Park im. dra H. Jordana
One of Poland's oldest children's parks, adjacent to Błonia meadow — dedicated sports and play areas, a large playground, and wide paths. Named after Dr Henryk Jordan who campaigned for children's recreation spaces in the 19th century. Very popular with local families on weekends.
OutdoorFree👶 Purpose-built for kids
🌊
Vistula Riverbank Walk
Bulwary Wiślane
From the dragon at Wawel all the way to Kazimierz — a lovely flat stroller walk along the river. In May the opposite bank is green and lush. Duck feeding spots, occasional food trucks, and beautiful castle views from below.
OutdoorFree👶 Duck spotting!
🌷
Kraków Botanical Garden
Ogród Botaniczny UJ
One of the oldest botanical gardens in Poland, founded in 1783 and run by the Jagiellonian University. In May it's absolutely at its best — flowering trees, tulips, rose beds, and a gorgeous palm greenhouse. Wide smooth paths make it ideal for a slow stroller morning. Toddlers love the ducks in the pond, the lily pads in the greenhouse, and the sheer variety of flowers. A peaceful, unhurried alternative to the busier sights.
Outdoor Free on selected weekdays 👶 Pond, ducks & greenhouse
📍 ul. Kopernika 27 · 15 min walk east of Rynek · Daily 9:00–19:00 in May Adult ~10 PLN · Child ~5 PLN · Under 7 free
🌲
Las Wolski Forest Park
Las Wolski
A vast urban forest on Kraków's western edge — 400 hectares of mixed woodland with shaded walking trails, cycling paths, and total quiet. The Kraków Zoo sits right inside it, so you can combine a forest walk with a zoo visit in one outing. The Piłsudski Mound at the forest's edge is easy to climb and gives lovely views. Bring a picnic, pack light layers, and let the little one lead the way through the trees. Feels miles from the city even though it's 40 minutes by tram.
Outdoor Free 👶 Forest trails + zoo combo
📍 Tram 18 to Salwator, then bus 134 · ~40 min from centre

Rainy Day Plan

Kraków's indoor options are fewer than Warsaw's, but still solid. The salt mine is the ultimate rainy-day escape.

🌧

Honest note on indoor options

Kraków doesn't have anything like Warsaw's Copernicus Science Centre. On a full rainy day, the salt mine is the best option — it's underground so rain is irrelevant. Otherwise, combine the Cloth Hall market, a milk bar lunch, and a museum to fill a wet afternoon.

⛏
Wieliczka Salt Mine
Completely underground — rain is 100% irrelevant. A truly extraordinary place. Take the Gnomes' Route for younger children — shorter, more child-friendly, with fairy-tale themed rooms carved in salt. Book in advance.
Book online at wieliczka-saltmine.com
Adult ~89 PLN · Child ~72 PLN
🎭
Groteska Puppet Theatre
Kraków's beloved puppet and animation theatre — performances designed for young children, often visually rich enough to transcend the language barrier. One of the best in Poland for the 2–6 age range.
Check programme at groteska.pl · Performances usually at weekends
Tickets from ~20 PLN
🦕
Natural History Museum
Dinosaur skeletons, minerals, stuffed animals, and fossils — the classic natural history museum formula that reliably works with toddlers. Compact and manageable in 1.5 hours.
ul. Świętosławska 17 · Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00
Adult ~15 PLN · Child ~8 PLN
🎨
Kraków Cloth Hall Market
The covered Sukiennice is a genuine dry-weather retreat — browse wooden toys, hand-painted crafts and amber. Buying your toddler a small wooden dragon here is almost obligatory. The market stays lively even in rain.
Centre of Rynek Główny · Daily 10:00–18:00
Free entry · Goods from ~5 PLN
☕
Kazimierz Café Crawl
Kazimierz is full of cosy, bohemian cafés with welcoming atmospheres — many have sofas, board games, and are completely relaxed about small children. A slow morning here with good coffee and pierogi while rain patters outside is genuinely lovely.
ul. Szeroka · Plac Nowy area · Many options
Coffee ~12 PLN · Cake ~15 PLN
🏰
Wawel Royal Chambers
The interior of the Royal Castle — state rooms, a dragon exhibition, and crown jewels. Stroller-accessible. Best visited during a shower. Buy tickets in advance; they sell out. Good for 1.5–2 hours.
Tue–Sun from 9:00 · Book at wawel.krakow.pl
Adult 35 PLN · Child 25 PLN · Free Sun (limited tickets)
⚔️
Rynek Underground Museum
A fascinating museum directly beneath Market Square — medieval market stalls, archaeological finds, and an interactive journey through Kraków's early history. Stroller-accessible via lift. Well-presented and engaging even for non-historians. Good for about 1.5 hours; combine with a hot drink in the Cloth Hall afterwards.
📍 Entrance through Cloth Hall · Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00 · Free Tuesdays
Adult 24 PLN · Child 18 PLN · Free Tue
🎓
Collegium Maius
The oldest surviving university building in Poland — a stunning Gothic courtyard with arcaded galleries. The treasury tour (~45 min) includes medieval globes, royal mementos, and astronomical instruments. The real toddler draw: the mechanical clock above the entrance plays a short musical performance with figurines at full hours — a reliable hit for little ones.
📍 ul. Jagiellońska 15 · Courtyard free to enter · Tours from 10:00
Adult 18 PLN · Child 12 PLN · Courtyard free
🐉
Dragon's Den (indoor option!)
The Smocza Jama cave is a real underground cave carved into Wawel Hill — and since it's inside a hill, rain is totally irrelevant. Descend 135 spiral steps into the dragon's lair, walk through the cave passage, and emerge at the fire-breathing dragon on the Vistula bank. The cave section itself takes about 20 minutes. Stroller stays at the top — carry or use a carrier for the descent.
📍 Base of Wawel Hill · Daily Apr–Oct · Closes at dusk
5 PLN per person · Under 7 free

Food & Drink

Kraków is a great food city — same Polish staples as Warsaw but with a slightly more artisanal, café-culture vibe, especially in Kazimierz.

Must-eat in Kraków

🥨
Obwarzanek Krakowski
Obwarzanek
The obwarzanek actually originates from Kraków — a ring-shaped boiled bread roll with sesame, poppy seed, or salt. Sold from distinctive round street carts throughout the city. A Kraków institution since the 14th century. Your toddler will demand one at every cart (2–3 PLN each).
The red wheeled carts are everywhere — you literally cannot miss them
🥟
Pierogi
Pierogi
Same beloved dumplings as Warsaw — Kraków has many excellent pierogi restaurants. Ruskie (potato & cheese) are the classic; try them both boiled and fried. Universally toddler-approved. Restauracja Polskie Smaki and Milkbar Tomasza are local favourites.
Try "z kapustą i grzybami" — sauerkraut and mushroom filling
🥣
Żurek w Chlebie
Żurek w Chlebie
Kraków's version of żurek is often served inside a hollowed bread bowl — sour rye soup with a hard-boiled egg and white sausage. The bread bowl makes it especially fun (and messy) for little ones to investigate. A quintessential Polish meal.
Order at Milkbar Tomasza or any traditional restaurant
🥪
Zapiekanka from Plac Nowy
Zapiekanka
Kraków's Plac Nowy in Kazimierz is the legendary home of zapiekanka — the open-faced baguette with mushrooms, cheese and toppings. The round market building (Okrąglak) has stalls on all sides serving them for ~10–15 PLN. Late-night student food that's somehow perfect at noon with a toddler.
Plac Nowy in Kazimierz · The real Kraków experience
🍦
Lody (Ice Cream)
Lody rzemieślnicze
Kraków's artisan ice cream scene is excellent — several great lodziarnie around the Rynek. On a May afternoon, queues form quickly. Flavours include classic śmietankowe (cream), owoce leśne (forest fruits), and seasonal specials.
Lody Tradycyjne on Floriańska is a local favourite
🍵
Kazimierz Café Culture
Kawiarnie
Kazimierz has some of the best cafés in Poland — artisan coffee, homemade cakes, and bohemian interiors. Alchemia, Singer, and Café Bunkier are Kraków institutions. Very relaxed about small children. Essential for parental sanity.
Szeroka St and Plac Nowy area have the highest density
🧀
Oscypek
Oscypek
Smoked sheep's cheese from the Tatra mountains — firm, salty, and deeply smoky, usually grilled and served with cranberry jam. Sold by highland vendors (Górale) in traditional dress at market stalls around the city. One of Poland's most distinctive and protected regional products. Kids love watching it get grilled on the spot.
Look for the highland stalls near the Cloth Hall and Planty gardens
🧇
Gofry (Polish Waffles)
Gofry
Thick Belgian-style waffles sold from street stalls throughout the city. Topped with whipped cream, fresh strawberries, Nutella, or jam. Practically the official street food of Polish children — any toddler who spots a gofry stall will not walk past without one.
Stalls all around the Market Square and Planty park — impossible to miss · ~8–12 PLN
🍲
Bigos
Bigos
Poland's legendary hunter's stew — slow-cooked sauerkraut and fresh cabbage with mixed meats, mushrooms, prunes, and spices. Rich, warming, and deeply satisfying on a cool May evening. Each cook has their own secret recipe; no two pots are the same. Order it at any traditional restaurant.
Order at Wesele, Polskie Smaki, or any milk bar — served with dark bread
🌭
Kiełbasa z Grilla
Kiełbasa z Grilla
Grilled Polish sausage — smoky, juicy, served with mustard and a crusty roll from outdoor grills at the Vistula riverfront and weekend markets. One of the most satisfying cheap street foods in Kraków. Toddlers who are past the dumpling phase will convert immediately to kiełbasa.
Best at the Vistula riverfront grills on warm May evenings · ~10–15 PLN
🛒

Best street food spots at a glance

Obwarzanek — red wheeled carts everywhere, especially Rynek & Planty · Zapiekanka — Plac Nowy Okrąglak, Kazimierz · Oscypek — highland stalls near Cloth Hall · Gofry — stalls all around Rynek and Planty · Kiełbasa — Vistula riverfront grills · Lody — Lody Tradycyjne, ul. Floriańska

Family-friendly restaurants

Pierogi specialist · milk bar
Milkbar Tomasza
Beloved milk bar on ul. Tomasza — authentic, cheap, excellent pierogi and żurek. Queue moves fast, atmosphere is no-nonsense and wonderful. Very toddler-tolerant.
📍 ul. Tomasza 24 · Cash preferred · Lunch ~20–30 PLN/person
Grandma's kitchen · super authentic
Kuchnia u Babci Maliny
One of Kraków's most beloved institutions — homestyle Polish cooking in a cosy kitchen decorated with grandma's memorabilia. Huge portions, tiny prices, and the kind of pierogi and bigos that taste like someone's grandmother made them. Often queues — absolutely worth it. Very welcoming to children.
📍 ul. Sławkowska 17 · No reservations · Lunch ~25–35 PLN/person
Tram depot brewery · most family-friendly
Stara Zajezdnia
A stunning restaurant inside a converted 19th-century tram depot in Kazimierz — brick arches, original tram tracks in the floor, and their own craft brewery. Huge space with plenty of room for strollers and restless toddlers. Polish classics plus burgers and salads. One of the most atmospheric and family-tolerant restaurants in the city. Perfect for a long Saturday lunch.
📍 ul. św. Wawrzyńca 12, Kazimierz · Reservations recommended · Mains 40–70 PLN
Buffet salad bar · great for picky eaters
Chimera Salad Bar
A Kraków institution in a vaulted cellar just off the Market Square — pay by weight at a huge buffet of salads, hot dishes, and soups. Perfect for families with a fussy toddler since everyone picks exactly what they want. Cheap, fast, and the cellar atmosphere is charming even for little ones.
📍 ul. św. Anny 3 · Daily 9:00–22:00 · ~20–35 PLN/person
Traditional Polish wedding feast
Wesele
Themed as a traditional Polish village wedding feast — folk décor, warm lighting, and an outstanding menu of bigos, pierogi, and roast meats. One of the best traditional Polish dining experiences in Kraków, still completely relaxed about children. Reserve ahead for dinner.
📍 Rynek Główny 10 · Reservations recommended · Mains 50–80 PLN
Traditional Polish · near Rynek
Restauracja Polskie Smaki
Classic Polish cuisine in a warm, rustic setting steps from the Market Square. Wide menu covering bigos, pierogi, kotlet schabowy, żurek — all done well. Staff experienced with families and portions are generous.
📍 Near Rynek Główny · Book ahead at weekends
Kazimierz · legendary street food square
Plac Nowy Food Scene
The round Okrąglak market building at Plac Nowy has zapiekanka stalls all around its perimeter — sit on a bench, watch neighbourhood life, and eat for under 15 PLN. On weekends a flea market fills the square. One of the most authentic and cheap eating experiences in Kraków.
📍 Plac Nowy, Kazimierz · From late morning · ~10–15 PLN/zapiekanka

Getting Around

Kraków's Old Town is almost entirely pedestrianised. You'll walk far more than you ride — and that's a good thing.

👟
Walking (primary mode)
The entire Old Town + Wawel + Kazimierz triangle is walkable. Old Town to Wawel: 10 min. Wawel to Kazimierz along the river: 15 min. Most cobblestones are manageable with a stroller. The Planty ring has smooth paths throughout.
Free · Best way to explore
🚋
Trams
Kraków's tram network covers the whole city well. No metro (unlike Warsaw). Trams have stroller space — board at middle doors. Useful for Błonia meadow, the zoo, and Wieliczka bus connection. Route planner: jakdojade.pl works for Kraków too.
Single ticket 4.80 PLN · 24h pass 18 PLN · 72h pass 44 PLN
🎫
Tickets
Buy at tram stops, Żabka convenience stores, or via the MKK app. Children under 4 travel free. Validate on board. Day passes are good value if you're going to the zoo or salt mine.
72h pass 44 PLN · Under 4: free!
🚖
Bolt / Uber
Both work well in Kraków. Useful for the salt mine trip, zoo, or when it's raining and you're far from a tram stop. Generally cheaper than Warsaw. No reliable child seat provision — bring a travel booster if needed.
City trips typically 15–25 PLN
🚌
Wieliczka Bus
Bus 304 from Kraków Główny (main train station) runs every 20 min to Wieliczka Salt Mine. Journey takes about 30 min. Buy ticket in advance. The bus stop is clearly signed at the station.
Regular tram ticket valid · or buy at station
🗺

Stroller in Kraków

The Old Town cobblestones are old and uneven in places — a lightweight 3-wheeler or all-terrain buggy handles them much better than a city stroller. The Planty park paths are smooth. Wawel Hill has ramps alongside the steps. The Dragon's Den requires carrying the child — leave the stroller at the top with one parent.
