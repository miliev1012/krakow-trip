Warsaw with the Family

Exploring the Polish capital with two adults and a 3-year-old — on foot, metro, tram and bus. No car needed.

May 6 – 11, 2026 · 5 nights Adults × 2 · Toddler 3yo No car — public transport ~15–20°C · Possible rain, pack layers

Trip at a Glance

Warsaw is more family-friendly than its reputation suggests — excellent public transport, beautiful parks, and a rebuilt Old Town that's very walkable with a stroller.

When

May 6–11, 2026
5 nights. May is ideal — long days, flowers in bloom, fewer tourists than summer.

Weather

Typically 15–20°C by day, 8–12°C at night. Spring showers are common — pack a light rain jacket and layers. Sunset around 8:30 pm.

Getting Around

Metro (2 lines), trams, and buses cover everything. Buses and trams have stroller bays — no folding required. 72h pass ≈ 36 PLN.

Currency

Polish złoty (PLN). As of 2026 ≈ 4.2 PLN = 1 EUR. Cards widely accepted; carry small cash for ice cream and markets.

Language

Polish. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. Learn a few words — Poles appreciate the effort.

Toddler Rating

Warsaw is very stroller-friendly. Łazienki Park, the riverfront, Old Town squares — all excellent for little explorers.

🗝

Buy the Warsaw Tourist Card

The 72h City Card (≈ 179 PLN/adult) includes unlimited transport + discounts on major museums. Compare with buying individual tickets — worth it if you plan to visit 3+ paid attractions.

🧒

Toddler logistics

Most museums offer reduced or free entry under 3 (Warsaw Zoo: 1 PLN under 3!). Łazienki Park has multiple playgrounds. Plan a mid-afternoon nap break — keeps everyone sane. Supermarkets (Biedronka, Żabka) are everywhere for snacks and milk.

☀️

Best thing about May in Warsaw

Free Chopin piano concerts in Łazienki Park every Sunday (noon & 4 pm) — May 10 falls on a Sunday. The park is full of peacocks, squirrels, and a gorgeous lake-side palace. Don't miss it.

Must-See Attractions

The big sights — all reachable by metro, tram or bus. Each one rated for 3-year-old engagement.

🏰
Old Town & Castle Square
Stare Miasto · Plac Zamkowy
Warsaw's UNESCO-listed Old Town was rebuilt stone-by-stone after WWII and is stunning. Start at Castle Square with the Sigismund's Column, stroll colourful Market Square, feed pigeons, and let the little one run across the cobblestones. Totally flat and manageable with a buggy.
👶 Toddler loves it Outdoor Free 📍 10 min walk from Centrum metro · Always open
🔭
Copernicus Science Centre
Centrum Nauki Kopernik
Warsaw's best hands-on museum — hundreds of interactive exhibits across physics, biology, and technology. Many are accessible to 3-year-olds (water tables, light experiments, giant pendulums). Book online — it sells out. Free on Tuesdays (check for time-slot availability). The attached planetarium is worth adding.
👶 Toddler loves it Indoor 📲 Book ahead ⭐ Don't miss
Tue–Sun 10:00–20:00 · Closed Mon Adult 31–33 PLN · Child 21–22 PLN · Under 3: FREE · Tue: FREE
⚠ Go at opening (10am) or after 11am — peak time is a scrum for toddlers
🦚
Łazienki Royal Park
Łazienki Królewskie
Warsaw's most beautiful park — 76 hectares of gardens, a lake-side palace, and wild peacocks roaming freely. Kids go nuts for the peacocks, squirrels, and ducks. Multiple playgrounds, wide paths, and easy bus access. Chopin concerts every Sunday under the monument (noon & 4 pm, May–September) — free and magical.
👶 Toddler loves it Outdoor Free entry ⭐ Sunday concert!
📍 Bus 116 or 180 from Castle Square · 10 min · Open dawn–dusk
🎹 Sunday May 10 — Chopin concert at noon & 4 pm — perfect timing!
🦁
Warsaw Zoo
Ogród Zoologiczny
One of Europe's most affordable and well-regarded zoos — 40 hectares, 12,000+ animals including polar bears, giraffes, and sloths. Very buggy-friendly paths. The recently renovated elephant house and tropical butterfly house are absolute hits. Pack a picnic lunch. Allow 3–4 hours minimum.
👶 Toddler loves it Outdoor ⭐ Don't miss
📍 Tram 4 or 26 to Zoo stop · Right bank of Vistula (Praga) Adult 60 PLN · Child (3+) 35 PLN · Under 3: only 1 PLN 🎉
⛲
Multimedia Fountain Park
Park Fontann
A huge outdoor fountain park near the Old Town with dramatic water, light and music shows on weekend evenings (May–Sep, from 8:30 pm). During the day it's a pleasant free space to wander and splash. Evening shows are spectacular and toddlers who can stay up love them.
Outdoor Free
📍 Walking distance from Old Town · Evening shows Fri–Sun from 20:30
🌊
Vistula Riverfront
Bulwary Wiślane
Warsaw's gorgeous riverside promenade stretching several kilometres. Street food, cafés on boats, pop-up bars, kids playing, and a relaxed local vibe. Great for a long stroll with the buggy, especially on warm May evenings. The Left Bank (below Old Town) is the liveliest stretch.
Outdoor Free 👶 Easy with stroller
📍 Walk down from Old Town (5 min) · Along ul. Wybrzeże Kościuszkowskie
🏛
POLIN Museum of the History of Polish Jews
Muzeum Historii Żydów Polskich POLIN
One of Europe's finest history museums — immersive, beautifully designed, and deeply moving. Best for parents during toddler nap time (if you have a carrier) or on a day when your little one is in good spirits and you have another adult to tag-team. The modern architecture alone is worth a look.
Indoor ⭐ Award-winning
Mon, Thu 10:00–18:00 · Wed, Fri 10:00–20:00 · Sat–Sun 10:00–18:00 · Closed Tue Adult 35 PLN · Under 7: FREE
🏙
Palace of Culture & Science
Pałac Kultury i Nauki (PKiN)
Warsaw's iconic Stalinist skyscraper and love-hate symbol. Take the lift to the observation deck on the 30th floor for panoramic city views. The surrounding area (Centrum) is a lively urban hub with shopping, cafés, and the main railway station. The building itself is free to look at — observation deck has a fee.
Indoor Outdoor views
📍 Plac Defilad 1 · Metro Centrum · Observation deck daily 10:00–20:00 Adult 30 PLN · Child 20 PLN
⚔️
Warsaw Rising Museum
Muzeum Powstania Warszawskiego
A powerful, immersive museum dedicated to the 1944 Warsaw Uprising. One of the best war museums in Europe — the emotional impact is extraordinary. Not tailored for toddlers content-wise, but very stroller-accessible. Best visited by parents in shifts if the little one needs attention. Allow 2–3 hours.
Indoor
Mon, Wed, Fri 8:00–18:00 · Thu 8:00–20:00 · Sat–Sun 10:00–18:00 · Closed Tue Adult 30 PLN · Child 20 PLN · Free on Sundays!
🏘
Praga District
Praga
Warsaw's hip right-bank neighbourhood — one of the few areas that survived WWII relatively intact. Peeling Art Nouveau facades, street art, independent cafés, and the famous Bazar Różyckiego market. A lovely half-day wander; very different vibe from the left bank. Home to the Zoo too.
Outdoor Free
📍 Tram 4, 26 or metro M2 to Dworzec Wileński

Parks, Playgrounds & Outdoor Play

Warsaw has excellent green spaces — all free, all stroller-friendly, and most have dedicated toddler play zones.

🦚
Łazienki Royal Park
Łazienki Królewskie
The crown jewel. 76 hectares of parkland with a lake-side neo-classical palace, rose garden, an open-air amphitheatre, and roaming peacocks. There are playgrounds with age-segmented areas — the toddler section is excellent. Wide, flat paths throughout. On Sundays in May: free Chopin concerts at the monument (noon & 4 pm).
👶 Top pick Outdoor Free ⭐ Chopin Sun May 10
📍 Bus 116, 180 from Plac Zamkowy · 10 min
🌳
Saxon Garden
Ogród Saski
Warsaw's oldest public park, right in the city centre. Formal French-style garden with fountains, sculptures, and shaded alleys. The fountain area is a classic meeting point and great for a stroll between Old Town and Centrum. Tomb of the Unknown Soldier is here. Small playground nearby.
Outdoor Free 👶 Easy stroller walk
📍 Plac Piłsudskiego · 10 min walk from Old Town
🌊
Vistula Beach & Riverfront Play
Plaża Miejska · Bulwary Wiślane
In summer the city opens pop-up sand beaches on the Vistula bank. In May it's quieter but the promenade is lovely — kids can splash in spray fountains along the bulwary. Sand castle potential near the beach area (Plaża Saska) on the right bank. Long flat promenade perfect for scooters and buggies.
Outdoor Free 👶 Splash & run
🌼
Pole Mokotowskie Park
Park Pole Mokotowskie
A huge open park in south Warsaw — popular with local families for picnics, kite-flying, and cycling. Great playground, a small pond, and food stalls on weekends. Less touristy than Łazienki; gives you a feel for everyday Warsaw life. Take the metro or tram.
Outdoor Free 👶 Playground + picnic
📍 Metro M1 Pole Mokotowskie · or tram 17 to Batorego
🧱
Klockownia — Block Play Centre
Klockownia
An indoor play space specifically designed around building blocks and construction for preschool-age children. DUPLO, wooden blocks, foam bricks — this is 3-year-old heaven. Perfect for a half-day when energy is high and outdoor weather is iffy. Calm and educational; not the chaos of a trampoline park.
👶 Perfect for 3yo Indoor
Entry approx. 35–40 PLN/child · parents usually free

Rainy Day Plan

May in Warsaw can surprise you with showers. Here's a full menu of indoor options — all excellent with a 3-year-old.

🌧

Rain is not a problem

Warsaw's indoor attractions are genuinely world-class. A rainy day could be the best excuse to spend the full day at the Copernicus Science Centre — it easily fills 5–6 hours. Book tickets online the night before.

🔭
Copernicus Science Centre
Hundreds of hands-on exhibits across multiple floors. Water, light, biology, tech — most are accessible to curious 3-year-olds. The most complete rainy-day option in Warsaw.

Warsaw Zoo — Indoor Pavilions
Even in rain, the zoo is doable — the tropical butterfly house, elephant building, and reptile house are fully indoors. A family poncho and wellies turn a drizzly day into a fun adventure.

The beautifully reconstructed royal residence in Old Town — gilded rooms, royal portraits, and Canaletto's famous Warsaw paintings. Good for about 90 minutes with a toddler who's in the carrier.

Lalka Puppet Theatre
Warsaw's beloved puppet theatre (Teatr Lalka) runs performances suitable for ages 2+. Check the schedule for May — productions are often in Polish but the visual storytelling transcends language perfectly for toddlers.

Not glamorous, but practical — a huge central mall next to Central Station with a food court, play area, and everything you might need. Good emergency option when rain is relentless and everyone needs a break and a hot meal.

Children's Corner Cafés
Warsaw has a growing café culture with dedicated children's corners and play nooks. Look for Spółdzielnia Ogniwo or Filtry Cafe — cosy spaces where parents get good coffee and kids get toys. Perfect for a slow morning.

National Museum
One of Poland's largest museums — ancient art, medieval, and modern sections. The Egyptian mummy collection is surprisingly engaging for kids. The Faras Gallery (Nubian medieval art) is unique in Europe.

Food & Drink

Polish cuisine is hearty, flavourful, and surprisingly toddler-friendly. These are the things you must eat.

Polish dishes to try

🥟
Pierogi
Pierogi
Poland's beloved dumplings — stuffed with potato & cheese (ruskie), meat, mushroom & cabbage, or sweet with blueberries. Served boiled or fried. Universal toddler hit.
Order "ruskie" or "z kapustą i grzybami" — classic flavours
🍞
Obwarzanek Krakowski
Obwarzanek
Ring-shaped pretzel bread sold from street stalls throughout Warsaw. Sesame, poppy seed, or salt. Cheap (2–3 PLN), filling, and kids go wild for them. Your toddler will want one at every stall.
Buy from street vendors around Old Town Market Square
🥣
Żurek
Żurek
Sour rye soup served in a bread bowl with a hard-boiled egg and white sausage. One of Poland's most iconic flavours — warming, earthy, and completely unlike anything you've had. Parents will love it; adventurous toddlers might too.
Order at any milk bar or traditional Polish restaurant
🥞
Naleśniki
Naleśniki
Polish crepes — thin, rolled, and filled with sweet tvaroh cheese with raisins, or jam and cream. A favourite with young children and available everywhere. Great for breakfast or dessert.
Look for them at pierogi restaurants — Zapiecek chain is reliable
🌭
Zapiekanka
Zapiekanka
Poland's iconic street food — a toasted baguette half with mushrooms, melted cheese, and toppings. Cheap, filling, and sold from kiosks. Think open-faced pizza. Toddlers usually approve.
Best from street kiosks near Plac Zbawiciela or Old Town
🍦
Lody
Lody (ice cream)
Polish artisan ice cream is excellent — bold flavours, proper texture. Look for hand-scooped lodziarnie. Essential during any May afternoon walk. The obligatory end to every park visit.
Lody Rzemieślnicze stalls around Old Town and Śródmieście

★★★★☆

🌊 Best on a warm May evening · Mostly card payments

Hala Koszyki
★★★★☆
A beautifully restored Art Nouveau market hall in Śródmieście with dozens of food stalls — everything from pierogi to sushi to craft beer. The hall is airy and kid-friendly; perfect for a lazy Sunday lunch when everyone wants something different.

Getting Around

No car needed — Warsaw's public transport is excellent, stroller-friendly, and very affordable.

🚇
Metro
Two lines: M1 (north–south, red) and M2 (east–west, blue). M2 connects Centrum with Praga (Zoo) and National Stadium area. Modern, fast, lifts at every station — great with a stroller.
Single ticket 3.40 PLN · 20-min ticket 3.40 PLN
🚌
Bus & Tram
Extensive network covers all attractions. Dedicated stroller spaces — you don't need to fold it. Night buses also run. Stop info on screens at every stop. Route planner: jakdojade.pl or Google Maps.
Same ticket as metro · Validate on board
🎫
Best Ticket Options
24h pass: 15 PLN
72h pass: 36 PLN
90-min ticket: 4.40 PLN
Children under 4 travel free! Buy at machines at metro stations, Żabka stores, or the ZTM app.
72h pass is best value for your trip
🚖
Bolt / Uber
Both apps work well in Warsaw. Useful for late evenings, heavy luggage days, or when your toddler is asleep in the stroller. Bolt is usually cheaper. Child seats not guaranteed — bring a lightweight travel one if needed.
Typical journey 15–30 PLN across the city
🚲
Nextbike / Veturilo
Warsaw's public bike share. Useful for adults for quick hops but not practical with a 3-year-old unless you bring a child seat. Lots of dedicated bike lanes — pleasant for parents who want to cycle alongside the buggy.
First 20 min free with registration
🗺

🧒

Stroller tips

All metro stations have lifts. Buses and trams have designated stroller areas — board at the middle doors, tap your ticket, park the buggy and hit the brake. Old Town streets are cobblestone — manageable but bumpy. A lightweight 3-wheel jogger stroller handles Warsaw much better than a big travel system.

📱

App to download before you go

jakdojade.pl is the definitive Warsaw public transport journey planner — more accurate than Google Maps for local buses and trams. Also download ZTM Warsaw for buying tickets in-app without hunting for a machine.
